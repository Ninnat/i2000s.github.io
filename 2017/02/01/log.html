Readings

  • “A Survey on Representation Theory”, Aguilar 2015
  • “Introduction to Categories” in Introduction to Representation Theory, Etingof et al.
  • “Homogeneous Vector Bundles and Induced Representations”, “Induced representations and Frobenius Reciprocity” and “The Peter-Weyl Theorem” in Lie Groups and Representations: Math G4344, Woit; Induced representations are important in (noncommutative) harmonic analysis on homogeneous spaces (e.g. spheres) and Mackey’s quantization based on system of imprimitivity
  • “Generalizations of entanglement based on coherent states and convex sets”, Barnum, Knill, Ortiz and Viola 2003
  • “Introduction to Symplectic Manifolds”; moment maps map coordinates on a phase space to conserved quantities that are more general than those that can be obtained by Noether’s theorem from the configuration space alone. The cross product formula for angular momenta \(\bold{q \times p = L}\) is an example of moment maps.

December

Books and focused readings

Books

  • An Introduction to Quantum Field Theory, Michael Peskin and Daniel Schroeder 1995; (Chapter 1-7) Pros: 1. Jumps right into QFT 2. Has everything, as it is often cited by other textbooks. Cons: 1. Jumps right into QFT without a preparation on special relativity and representation theory 2. Has everything but you can’t find it until you already know what to look for. Hard to grasp a big picture
  • A Modern Introduction to Quantum Field Theory, Michele Maggiore 2005; Cons: Does not jump right into QFT. Pros: 1. Does not jump right into QFT but prepare a reader with special relativity and representation theory 2. Concise and modern (teaches the Wilsonian viewpoint of renormalization with block spin renormalization)
  • Gauge Fields, Knots and Gravity, John Baez and Javier Muniain 1994 (Part 1-2); Strike a good balance between rigorous math and physicists’ math. Double as a quick introduction to differential geometry
  • “Chern-Simons quantum field theories” in Introduction to Topological Quantum Computation, Pachos 2012

Readings

  • “Phase space formulation of quantum mechanics and quantum state reconstruction of physical systems with Lie-group symmetries”, Brif and Mann 1999

These solutions to the Yang–Baxter equation give rise to new families of commuting transfer matrices, and therefore to new exactly solvable models describing new types of phase transitions.”
